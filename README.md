# Rigorous Grade 3 Summer Math

A complete 10-week summer mathematics program for a rising third grader, with printable student packets, scripted teacher guides, assessments, and problem-solving enrichment.

The curriculum uses Florida's B.E.S.T. Standards as a baseline while extending beyond minimum grade-level expectations through conceptual models, mathematical reasoning, challenging problems, and preparation for later competition mathematics and standardized tests.

## Who This Is For

- Families teaching mathematics at home during the summer.
- Tutors working with a rising third grader.
- Teachers looking for structured review, acceleration, or enrichment materials.
- Students who need both a strong Grade 3 foundation and room to think deeply.

The program assumes an adult will teach or facilitate each lesson. Teacher guides include the background and language needed for an adult who is not a mathematics specialist.

## What Is Included

- 10 weeks of instruction.
- 5 lessons per week.
- 50 student lesson packets in DOCX and PDF formats.
- 50 scripted teacher guides in DOCX and PDF formats.
- Placement diagnostic and final assessment.
- Weekly assessments with answer notes.
- Florida B.E.S.T. alignment guidance.
- Fluency, problem-solving, competition, and long-term SAT foundations.
- Reusable number lines, grids, fraction tools, and organizers.

Student packets are designed to feel inviting rather than worksheet-heavy. Each packet includes a mission, mathematical language, a worked example, guided work, independent practice, a challenge, an exit check, and a reusable math tool.

Teacher guides explain the mathematics, how to prepare and use materials, what to say, what to listen for, likely misconceptions, differentiation options, and how to assess the student's understanding.

1. Open the [Grade 3 Summer Math Program](grade_3_summer_math/README.md).

## Ten-Week Sequence

| Week | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Place value, number forms, comparison, rounding, and estimation |
| 2 | Multi-digit addition, subtraction, regrouping, and two-step problems |
| 3 | Equal groups, arrays, multiplication, and division foundations |
| 4 | Multiplication and division facts through 12 |
| 5 | Properties, variables, equations, and multi-step problems |
| 6 | Fractions, equivalence, comparison, and number lines |
| 7 | Measurement, elapsed time, data, graphs, mass, and volume |
| 8 | Geometry, quadrilaterals, area, and perimeter |
| 9 | Logic, patterns, estimation, counting, and non-routine problems |
| 10 | Cumulative review, final assessment, corrections, and Grade 4 preview |

See the full [Scope and Sequence](grade_3_summer_math/scope-and-sequence.md) for lesson-level details.

## Educational Approach

The program emphasizes:

- Understanding before memorization.
- Concrete, visual, and symbolic representations.
- Precise mathematical language.
- Multiple solution strategies.
- Daily explanation and justification.
- Productive struggle without unnecessary pressure.
- Fluency built from relationships among facts.
- Error analysis as part of learning.
- Challenging problems that develop flexible thinking.

Florida standards serve as the floor, not the ceiling. Extension work is included to deepen reasoning while keeping the core content developmentally appropriate.

## Important Notes

- Adapt pacing and number size to the individual student.
- Do not introduce speed pressure before strategies are secure.
- Accept mathematically valid methods that differ from the guide.
- Review current Florida standards and local school expectations when using the program for formal instruction.
