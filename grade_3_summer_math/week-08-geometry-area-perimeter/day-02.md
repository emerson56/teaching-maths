# Day 2: Area as Tiling

## Goal

Understand area as the number of square units needed to cover a flat region without gaps or overlaps.

## Materials

Square tiles, grid paper, irregular shapes.

## Number Talk

Which covers more space: a long skinny rectangle or a short wide rectangle? What information do you need?

## Main Lesson

1. Cover rectangles with square tiles.
2. Count tiles by rows instead of one by one.
3. Discuss why units must be equal squares.
4. Tile irregular rectilinear shapes.
5. Connect area to "covering" rather than "around."

## Practice

- Count area by tiling.
- Draw shapes with area 12 square units.
- Compare two shapes by area.
- Fix a tiling with gaps or overlaps.

## Challenge

Draw three different shapes with area 16 square units.

## Exit Check

Why is area measured in square units?
