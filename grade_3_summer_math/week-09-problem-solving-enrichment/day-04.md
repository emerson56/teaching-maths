# Day 4: Geometry and Fraction Puzzles

## Goal

Use spatial visualization, decomposition, symmetry, and fractions in non-routine puzzles.

## Materials

Tangrams, square tiles, fraction strips, grid paper.

## Number Talk

How many different ways can a square be divided into four equal areas?

## Main Lesson

1. Build a square from tangram pieces.
2. Identify fractional parts of the whole.
3. Rearrange pieces without changing total area.
4. Solve a perimeter puzzle on grid paper.
5. Discuss what changes and what remains invariant.

## Practice

- Complete two tangram silhouettes.
- Partition a rectangle into equal areas in three ways.
- Find a missing area.
- Solve one visual fraction comparison.

## Challenge

Move one square tile in a shape so the perimeter changes but the area stays the same.

## Exit Check

Name one quantity that stayed invariant during a puzzle.
