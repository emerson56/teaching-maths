# Teaching Guide

## Daily Lesson Shape

Total: 60-75 minutes.

1. Number talk: 5-8 minutes.
2. Fluency review: 8-10 minutes.
3. Main lesson: 25-30 minutes.
4. Practice: 15-20 minutes.
5. Reflection or exit check: 5 minutes.

## Teaching Sequence

Use concrete -> pictorial -> abstract.

- Concrete: objects, tiles, blocks, strips, clocks, money.
- Pictorial: drawings, arrays, bar models, number lines, area models.
- Abstract: equations, symbols, efficient procedures.

## Parent Prompts

Ask these constantly:

- What do you notice?
- Can you draw it?
- Is there another way?
- How do you know?
- Does your answer make sense?
- What mistake might someone make here?
- Can you make a simpler version of the problem?

## What Counts as Rigorous

Rigorous:

- Show two ways to solve `8 x 7`.
- Write a story problem for `48 / 6`.
- Find all rectangles with area 36.
- Compare `3/6` and `2/4` using a model.
- Explain why an answer is impossible.

Not rigorous by itself:

- Long worksheets of identical problems.
- Timed drills before conceptual strategies are strong.
- Pushing Grade 4 algorithms while Grade 3 reasoning is shaky.

## Protecting Love of Math

- Praise clear thinking, persistence, and elegant strategies more than speed.
- End many sessions with a puzzle, game, or surprising pattern.
- Keep a "beautiful problems" notebook.
- Let the student teach a solution back to you.
- Stop when frustration is no longer productive; return later with a simpler case.
