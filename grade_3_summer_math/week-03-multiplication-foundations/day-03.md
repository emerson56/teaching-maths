# Day 3: Skip-Counting and Table Patterns

## Goal

Connect skip-counting to multiplication and identify patterns in a multiplication table.

## Materials

Hundreds chart, blank multiplication table, colored pencils.

## Number Talk

What patterns do you notice in multiples of 2, 5, and 10?

## Main Lesson

1. Mark multiples of 2, 5, and 10 on a hundreds chart.
2. Connect the nth multiple to `n x factor`.
3. Explore even/odd results.
4. Color the diagonal square facts.
5. Find symmetry across the diagonal.

## Practice

- Complete selected rows and columns of a multiplication table.
- Fill missing values in skip-counting sequences.
- Explain why every multiple of 10 is also a multiple of 5.
- Decide whether claims about patterns are always, sometimes, or never true.

## Challenge

Find two different multiplication patterns that pass through 24 on the table.

## Exit Check

How does knowing `5 x 6` help find `6 x 5`?
