# Day 4: Facts for 11 and 12; Remainders

## Goal

Derive 11s and 12s and interpret remainders in context.

## Materials

Counters, array cards, grid paper.

## Number Talk

How can `10 x 8` help with `11 x 8` and `12 x 8`?

## Main Lesson

1. Derive 11s as 10 groups plus 1 group.
2. Derive 12s as 10 groups plus 2 groups.
3. Divide 43 counters into groups of 6.
4. Record `43 / 6 = 7 remainder 1`.
5. Discuss whether a context requires 7 groups, 8 groups, or a leftover.

## Practice

- Mixed 11 and 12 facts.
- `29 / 4`, `38 / 6`, and `50 / 8` with models.
- Explain what each remainder means in a story.

## Challenge

There are 43 students and each table seats 6. How many tables are needed? Explain why the answer is not simply 7 remainder 1.

## Exit Check

Write one context in which a remainder is discarded and one in which it causes rounding up.
