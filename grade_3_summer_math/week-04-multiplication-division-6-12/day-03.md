# Day 3: Division as an Unknown Factor

## Goal

Interpret division as sharing, grouping, and an unknown-factor problem.

## Materials

Counters, cups, array cards.

## Number Talk

If `7 x 8 = 56`, what are `56 / 7` and `56 / 8`?

## Main Lesson

1. Share 24 counters equally among 6 groups.
2. Make groups of 6 from 24 counters.
3. Compare the two division meanings.
4. Rewrite `32 / 8 = ?` as `8 x ? = 32`.
5. Use multiplication to check every quotient.

## Practice

- Model `35 / 5`, `42 / 7`, and `72 / 8`.
- Sort sharing and grouping stories.
- Solve missing divisors and missing dividends.

## Challenge

Write two different stories for `48 / 6`: one sharing and one grouping.

## Exit Check

Explain why `56 / 7 = 8` without using repeated subtraction.
