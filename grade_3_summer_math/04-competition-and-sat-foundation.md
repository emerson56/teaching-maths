# Competition and SAT Foundation

Do not make a rising 3rd grader study the SAT. Build the traits that later make SAT math and competition math feel natural.

## Weekly Competition Strand

Add two 20-30 minute sessions per week.

Session A: deep problem.

- Give one unfamiliar problem with no taught procedure.
- Allow 10-15 minutes of drawing, experimenting, and revising.
- Ask for a complete explanation.
- Discuss a second method if possible.

Session B: mathematical play.

- Use a logic puzzle, strategy game, spatial puzzle, number pattern, tangram, pentomino, or counting game.
- Keep score only if the child enjoys it.
- Stop while interest is still high.

## Skills to Rotate

- Pattern recognition.
- Logical deduction.
- Counting without double-counting.
- Spatial visualization.
- Invariants: what stays the same?
- Working backward.
- Making an organized list or table.
- Guess, check, and improve.
- Drawing a simpler case.
- Estimation and bounds.
- Explaining why an answer must be correct.

## Resource Progression

Grades 3-4:

- Beast Academy Levels 3 and 4.
- Math Kangaroo practice problems.
- MOEMS elementary problems when routine Grade 3 work is secure.
- Local math circles focused on exploration.

Grades 5-6:

- Continue Beast Academy, then transition selectively to Art of Problem Solving.
- Add number theory, combinatorics, geometry, and probability.
- Begin occasional AMC 8 problems by topic, without timed full tests.

Grades 7-8:

- Prepare systematically for AMC 8.
- Preserve school-course mastery in ratios, proportions, equations, geometry, and data.
- Use error logs and written solutions.

High school:

- Build rigorous Algebra I, Geometry, and Algebra II.
- Pursue AMC 10 if the student enjoys competition math.
- Begin SAT-specific practice only after the underlying algebra and data content is learned.

## SAT Foundations Built This Summer

- Algebra: missing numbers, equality, variables, patterns, distributive reasoning.
- Advanced math: structure and equivalent expressions.
- Problem-solving/data analysis: units, graphs, tables, estimation, multi-step contexts.
- Geometry: vocabulary, area, perimeter, decomposition, spatial reasoning.
- Test execution: careful reading, reasonableness checks, calm recovery after mistakes.
